Crimp Tool

I didn't know how to do this. I solved it the following way.

This project was interesting. The deadline was very tight, I had to finish it on short notice before leaving mainstream to return to school. Because of that, I didn’t get to build it or oversee its construction myself. Still, I think the problem required prior hands-on knowledge of fittings and plumbing because, without that, one could easily make this design very difficult or impossible to build. The problem was that we were creating a new, relatively small, production line for a run of units. These units demanded to be evacuated and charged with refrigerant at a point in the line, so at first we looked at purchasing an industry-standard machine. High-quality machines that perform this operation can reach $150,000. Naturally, we didn’t want to spend that money so we looked into producing our own. In theory, it was possible to replicate, so I took a large old electrical cabinet and had it emptied out to use as a pipe packaging container. That is what the pipes in the imagery below are packaged inside. Ultimately I predicted the cost of this to be in the low thousands which is a significant improvement over the other off-the-shelf options. Fortunately, we have another refrigerant fill system in use in another part of our facility, so I was able to take inspiration from that design for the process.
